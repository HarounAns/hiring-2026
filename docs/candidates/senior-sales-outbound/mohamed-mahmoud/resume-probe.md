### Where He's Weak (Probe These Hard)

| Concern | How to probe | Listen for |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| **Zero AI/LLM experience** | "This product is AI-native — the core is an LLM agent that has SMS conversations. Nothing on your resume touches AI or LLMs. What's your exposure?" | Honest self-awareness + curiosity is okay. "I can learn anything" without specifics is not. Has he built anything with LLMs on the side? Does he have intuition about prompting, evals, non-determinism? |
| **No product ownership** | "Every role on your resume is a team engineering role at a large company. Have you ever decided *what* to build — not how to build it, but what the product should do?" | If the answer is always "the PM scoped it" or "the team decided," that's the flag. You need someone who can look at a messy customer problem and define the solution themselves. |
| **No customer-facing work** | "This role involves talking to dealerships directly. A dealer emails you saying the AI booked wrong appointments all week. You own the conversation. When have you done something like that?" | Enterprise engineers often have zero direct customer exposure. Does he default to "escalate to PM/CS" or does he think about the customer's business? |
| **No startup/0→1 signal** | "Capital One, Accenture, Lumen — all large orgs with established products. What makes you want to leave that for a role where you're building alone with no guardrails?" | Running toward ownership vs. running away from corporate. "I want more impact" is vague. "I've been wanting to build X and couldn't" is specific. |
| **Resume is very generic** | "Your Capital One section says 'design and develop scalable backend microservices.' Walk me through one specific system — what it did, what decisions you made, what you'd change." | The resume has no metrics, no outcomes, no specific products. Every bullet could be on any engineer's resume. If he can't go deep on a specific system he built, that's a depth flag. |
| **No side projects visible** | "Do you build anything outside of work? Open source, side projects, anything?" | The 0→1 role needs someone who builds because they can't help it. If engineering is strictly 9-5 at corporate jobs, the founder energy may not be there. |
