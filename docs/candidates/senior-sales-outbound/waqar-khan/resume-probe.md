## 1. Intro + Context

- "This role is a founding engineer position for a new product. AI does outbound SMS to dealership leads, books appointments, syncs to their CRM. 40 dealers live, scaling to 200."
- "You'd own the entire product end-to-end. No PM, no spec, no team to manage — at least not for a while. It's just you building alone, talking to customers, shipping."

**Listen for:** Does he light up at "you're building alone" or does he start asking about team size, org structure, and roadmap process? The first reaction is everything.

## 2. Probe the Resume

### Where He's Strong (Confirm These Are Real)

| Lens | Resume signal | What to confirm |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Domain overlap** | Runs contact center engineering at Block. Built voice AI systems across Cash App, Square, Afterpay. Claims 30-40% autonomous resolution of support interactions. | What does "autonomous resolution" actually mean technically? Is this IVR decision trees and call routing, or actual AI-driven conversation? Did he build the system or manage the team that built it? |
| **Founder experience** | Monster Pool — fintech platform with wallets, debit cards, lending pools. Raising $1.6M pre-seed. | He ran this concurrently with Block full-time. How much did he actually build? Was he the technical founder writing code, or the business founder with contractors? What happened — why did it shut down? |
| **Scale understanding** | Global contact center ops across three major products (Cash App, Square, Afterpay). AWS ecosystem, Terraform, CI/CD. | This is systems-at-scale, which is table stakes. The question is whether he can go the other direction — start tiny and iterate. |

### Where He's Weak (Probe These Hard)

| Concern | How to probe | Listen for |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| **Manager for 5+ years — can he still build?** | "Your title is Sr. Engineering Manager and you've been in that role for 5 years. When's the last time you shipped production code? Not reviewed it — wrote it." | Honest answer about how long it's been. If it's been years since he coded, the 0→1 role is a hard reset. The role needs someone shipping production code on day one, not building a team and roadmap. |
| **Traditional voice AI, not LLM-based** | "Your Voice AI experience is Amazon Connect, Genesys, IVR systems. Our product is an LLM agent — it has real conversations over SMS using language models. That's a different world from IVR flows. What's your exposure to LLMs and conversational AI?" | Does he understand the difference between rule-based IVR and LLM-driven conversation? Has he built anything with LLMs, even on the side? "I've used ChatGPT" is not enough. Zero LLM experience is a real gap for this role. |
| **No product ownership signal** | "Every role on your resume is platform/infrastructure — building the tools that other teams use. Have you ever owned the end product that a customer interacts with? Not the platform underneath it — the actual product?" | Contact center engineering is internal tooling. The 0→1 role is customer-facing product ownership. Does he understand the difference? Can he point to a time he decided what to build based on what a customer needed? |
| **Monster Pool depth** | "Walk me through Monster Pool. You ran it for 3 years alongside a full-time job at Block. What did you actually build? What was the tech stack? How many users? Why did it end?" | Was he the engineer or the CEO? Did he write the code or outsource it? 3 years concurrent with a demanding full-time management role — how deep could he have gone? If he built it himself, that's strong 0→1 signal. If he managed contractors, it's a different story. |
| **Corporate resume language** | "Your Block section says 'provide essential technical leadership and guidance on different CCAAS voice platforms.' Walk me through one specific system you built — what it does, how it works, what decisions you made." | The resume is dense with corporate speak and zero specifics. Every bullet could be on any engineering manager's LinkedIn. Can he go deep on a specific technical system, or does he stay at the altitude of "stakeholder management and organizational development"? |
| **Stack mismatch** | "Our stack is TypeScript end-to-end — React frontend, Node.js backend, PostgreSQL, Kafka. Your resume is AWS infrastructure, Terraform, CCAAS platforms. How fast can you ship production TypeScript?" | His resume has no TypeScript, no React, no modern web stack. The 0→1 role can't wait for someone to learn a new stack. Does he have web development experience that didn't make the LinkedIn? |
| **Why IC, why now?** | "You've been an engineering manager for 5+ years. You manage teams, budgets, roadmaps. This role has none of that. Why do you want to go back to building?" | Is this genuine hunger to build, or is he between management roles and this seems interesting? "I miss coding" is okay. "I want to build something from scratch" is better. "I'm looking for a leadership role where I can also be hands-on" is a red flag — this role is 100% hands-on with no team. |
| **Relocation** | "You're in SF Bay Area. This role is in-person, DC area. Are you ready to relocate?" | Commitment vs hedging. |

## 3. Gut Checks

- "When's the last time you wrote and shipped production code — not managed a team doing it?"
- "Our product is an LLM SMS agent. Your background is IVR/CCAAS. What's your honest assessment of the gap?"
- "If you started Monday, 40 dealerships live, AI doing outbound SMS — what's your first week? And to be clear: there's no team. It's just you and the codebase."

## 4. His Questions

What he asks tells you everything. Product/customers/building = good. Team size, management scope, organizational structure, career progression into leadership = this is the wrong role for him.

## What Would Make This a Clear Yes to Technical

- Monster Pool was genuinely his build — he wrote the code, designed the product, talked to users
- Can go deep on a specific technical system he built (not managed) at Block or Square
- Has LLM experience that didn't make the LinkedIn — side projects, personal exploration, something
- Genuinely wants to go back to IC and build, not looking for a player-coach management role
- Can articulate why outbound AI is interesting to him specifically, not generically
- Honest about the stack gap with a credible ramp plan
- Ready to relocate

## What Would Make This a Quick No

- Hasn't written production code in years and can't point to anything recent
- Zero LLM exposure — doesn't understand the difference between IVR and conversational AI
- Monster Pool was a business venture, not a technical one — he didn't build the product
- Keeps gravitating toward management language — teams, roadmaps, org structure, stakeholder management
- Can't go beneath resume-level corporate bullets on any specific system
- Treats this as a stepping stone back to engineering management
- Hedges on relocation
