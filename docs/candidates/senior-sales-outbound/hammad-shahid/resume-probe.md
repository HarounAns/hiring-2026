## Recommended Questions (Pick 3-4)

1. **Open:** "You built ForemanAI — a voice agent that calls construction suppliers. Walk me through how it works. Has it actually called a supplier and gotten a real estimate back?"
   - This is the most interesting thing on the resume and the closest to what Pam builds. If he can go deep on how the voice agent handles real conversations, edge cases, failures — that's the signal you need.

2. **Ownership test:** "The reliability platform, the alert suppression system, the RAG post-mortem tool — you built a lot at Capital One. Which one was your idea from the start? Not assigned, not scoped by a PM — you saw a problem and decided to build the solution."
   - Distinguishes builder from executor. If everything was assigned, he's a strong engineer but maybe not an owner.

3. **Customer instinct:** "RentBack is live at rentback.nyc. Who uses it? Have you talked to renters? Has anyone actually negotiated their rent using it?"
   - Product instinct test. Did he build it for users or for his portfolio? The answer tells you whether he thinks in products or in projects.

4. **Gut check:** "If I dropped you into our codebase tomorrow — 40 dealerships live, AI doing outbound SMS, scaling to 200 — what's your first week?"
