# Pam's Core Values

### **1. Never disappoint the customer** ★

We take a service-oriented approach: highly attentive and highly responsive to our dealers. We use automations, software, and AI to proactively identify opportunities, solve issues before they become problems, and drive their success. Prioritize driving real value and direct engagement with customers.

*Right now, we're falling short. Integration setup P95 is 6 weeks. CDK averages 24 days. At Walser, an appraisal feature stopped working and nobody caught it for three weeks — hundreds of calls went through without the feature. At Fette Ford, Pam started pairing days of the week with dates from the wrong year. A CSM flagged it: "I've flagged this same issue at a number of different dealerships." Multiple stores, same bug, nobody connected the pattern. This role exists because we're disappointing our customers, and we need someone who feels the weight of that and builds the systems to prevent it.*

---

### **2. Owner's Mindset** ★

Everyone gives their best effort, takes full responsibility for outcomes, and takes pride in their work. No passengers, every role matters.

*This role demands full ownership because nobody else is doing it. You own the entire onboarding automation surface area — the integration playbook, the escalation system, the agentic workflows, the observability layer. End to end, it's yours. That's not just responsibility — it's leverage. If we can scale onboarding from 15 rooftops a week to 30+ without adding headcount, that fundamentally changes the economics of the business. The person who builds that system is the person who made it possible. That gets paid back.*

---

### 3. Share the truth when it hurts

We share openly, surface blockers quickly, and do not hide problems.

- Communicate early and often
- Prefer in-person communication
- When in doubt, use Slack channels, not DMs
- Do not let yourself stay blocked, insist on guidance or clarification

Do not let failure happen silently. It's okay to make mistakes and okay to fail. That's gonna happen as we push boundaries and move fast. What's important is that we learn from mistakes and failures and ensure it doesn't happen again.

---

### 4. Ship Fast With Urgency: Done is Better Than Perfect

We value momentum and iteration over slow perfectionism. We work with urgency to keep deals moving, customer issues solved, and projects unblocked.

Quality and quantity both matter. When in doubt, opt to do more than spend longer on a single thing.

---

### **5. Embrace AI: The Hardworker's Paradox** ★

We leverage AI to move faster, scale smarter, and deliver better results than competitors. And we stay in the driver's seat. AI is a tool; you are responsible for your output and can't make it your crutch.

*Your interview vision was conservative for someone known as the top AI user at HubSpot. At Pam, "Embrace AI" doesn't mean using Claude Code to write features faster. It means building systems that learn — self-learning investigation loops, agentic onboarding workflows, eval-gated deployments where the system proposes fixes, validates them in sandboxes, and compounds knowledge with every cycle. The people who thrive here don't ask "how do I automate this task?" — they ask "how do I build a system that gets smarter every time it runs?" We think Pam will stretch your vision to that level.*

---

### 6. Protect the Zone

We safeguard our time, focus, and momentum by minimizing distractions and keeping the team aligned on the highest-impact priorities.

---

### 7. Ruthless Prioritization

We invest in what directly drives dealership adoption, retention, and growth. Distractions do not survive here.

---

### 8. Principles over Profit

When faced with a business proposition that challenges our principles and values, we preserve our principles and seek opportunity elsewhere.

## My Worries

I've learned that the gap between using AI and building with AI is wider than most people think.

You're known as one of the top AI users at HubSpot. But your interviews showed a gap between that reputation and how boldly you think about what AI can do. Your solutions were practical and sound — in Interview 2, you designed a system that would genuinely improve our onboarding. But when Marcelo asked about the end state, your first instinct was to say "I'm not sure how much more automated we can make it." When I pushed you, you found the answer — Pam calling IT contacts directly to extract configuration data instead of waiting for humans to fill out forms. That answer was in you. It just wasn't your first instinct.

At Pam, that needs to be your first instinct.

We don't just use AI as a tool here — we build systems that learn. The key accounts investigation system is a real self-learning loop: alerts fire, AI investigates from first principles across six data sources, proposes fixes with confidence scores matched against a library of prior cases, and the human approves in minutes. The system discovers patterns nobody would have hypothesized. The V4 Operations Vision takes it further — systems that detect config drift before customers notice, propose prompt edits in sandboxes, run eval suites, and compound knowledge with every cycle. We're building things before they get big on Twitter. We built "skills" in a cabin before Anthropic started calling them skills.

Your pragmatism is valuable. We're not asking you to abandon it. We need you to develop a stronger vision *on top of* the practical foundation you already have. The people who grow fastest here are the ones who start with "what if the system could just handle this?" and then work backwards to what's shippable today.

We think being here — surrounded by Omer, Marcelo, and the rest of this team — will stretch your thinking to that level. That's not a risk we're worried about. It's the reason we want you here.
