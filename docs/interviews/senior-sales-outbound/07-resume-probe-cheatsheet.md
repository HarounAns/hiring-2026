# Resume Probe Cheat Sheet — Senior SWE 0→1

**15 min | You're filtering for: Ownership, 0-to-1 Builder, Product Thinker, AI-Native, Full-Stack**

This is the first half of the Culture / Resume Screen. The second half is the standard culture fit screen (see `culture-fit-cheat-sheet.md`).

---

## What You're Testing

**Can this person own a product, or do they need someone to tell them what to build?**

The 0→1 role is not a staff engineer role. It's not a "build features from tickets" role. The person taking this seat needs to look at a messy domain, decide what matters, build it, talk to customers, and iterate. The resume probe is your first signal for whether they've done that before — or whether they've only executed within structure someone else created.

---

## The 5 Lenses

Pull on whatever's on the resume, but filter everything through these:

| # | Lens | What you're looking for | Red flag |
|---|------|------------------------|----------|
| 1 | **0-to-1** | Built something from nothing — new product, new codebase, greenfield. Decided *what* to build, not just *how*. | All experience is extending/maintaining existing systems |
| 2 | **Ownership** | Owned outcomes, not tasks. Made product decisions. Pushed back on bad ideas. Chose what to build and what to skip. | "I was assigned to..." / "The PM decided..." / "My manager scoped it" |
| 3 | **Customer-facing** | Talked to real users/customers. Handled feedback. Made product calls based on what customers said. | Zero customer interaction across entire career |
| 4 | **AI depth** | Built with LLMs — agents, evals, prompt engineering, conversation design. Not just API calls. | "I've used ChatGPT" / no AI experience at all |
| 5 | **Scrappy / ambiguity** | Operated without specs, without a PM, without clear requirements. Figured it out. | Only big-company experience with defined processes |

---

## Flow

**Open with:**
> "Walk me through your resume — what's the thing you're most proud of building?"

Then pull the thread. Don't rapid-fire questions — go deep on 1-2 things rather than shallow on 5.

**For each thing they mention, ask yourself:** Which lenses does this hit? Which is it missing?

### Go-to follow-ups

| When they describe a project... | Ask this | You're testing |
|--------------------------------|----------|---------------|
| Sounds like they built something | "How much of that was you deciding what to build vs someone handing you a spec?" | Ownership vs execution |
| They led a team or product | "Give me an example where you pushed back on a request — from a customer, PM, or stakeholder." | Owner vs order-taker |
| They have AI/LLM experience | "What did you actually build with it? What broke? What was hard?" | Depth vs buzzwords |
| They worked at a big company | "What's the biggest difference between how you worked there and how you'd work at a 15-person startup?" | Self-awareness about the transition |
| They claim 0-to-1 | "When you started, what existed? What decisions did nobody else make for you?" | Real 0-to-1 vs inheriting something |
| Their resume is all remote | "This role is in-person, DC area. Are you ready to relocate?" | Practical filter |

### The gut-check question (use near the end)

> "If I dropped you into a codebase tomorrow with 40 live customers and said 'this is yours now, scale it to 200' — what's your first week look like?"

- **Great:** Asks questions first. Talks to customers. Reads the data. Understands before prescribing.
- **Bad:** Starts listing technical improvements without understanding the domain.

---

## Decision Framework

The resume probe doesn't produce a pass/fail. It gives you signal for the culture fit half.

| Signal | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| Strong on lenses 1-3 (0-to-1, ownership, customer) | This person might be an owner. Culture fit will confirm. |
| Strong on 4-5 (AI, scrappy) but weak on 1-3 | Good engineer, maybe wrong role. Could be a builder but not an owner. |
| Weak across the board | Culture fit is unlikely to save it. Consider ending early. |
| Resume doesn't match what they say | Dig harder. If the story changes under pressure, that's your answer. |

**You're not scoring the resume. You're calibrating how hard to push in the culture fit half.**

---

## After the Probe

Transition to culture fit:

> "Cool — I've got a good sense of your background. Let me tell you about us and what we're looking for."

Then run the standard 6 questions + oath from `culture-fit-cheat-sheet.md`.
