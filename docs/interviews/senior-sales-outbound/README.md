## Interview Philosophy

**This is one flowing session, not three separate exercises.**

You present a real product scenario — Pam's outbound sales system for auto dealerships — and see how they think through it across three phases:

1. **Model the domain** — Can they extract the right entities from a problem description? Both the CRM/data side and the AI agent configuration side?
2. **Operationalize it** — Can they take the model they just designed and automate provisioning it for 200 dealers?
3. **Own it** — When a dealer asks for a feature, do they ship it or test it? Can they build a system for continuous improvement?

**The meta-signal across all three: does this person think like an owner or like an implementer?**

The owner asks "what outcome are we trying to drive?" The implementer asks "what do you want me to build?"

**Format:**
- Share a Google Doc with the candidate
- Present the scenario verbally
- Let them write/sketch/talk through each phase
- Thread-pull based on what they write and what they miss

---

## The Interview Structure

| Phase | Duration | What You Say | What You're Testing |
|-------|----------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Setup** | 5 min | Frame the scenario | — |
| **Phase 1: Model the Domain** | 20 min | "How would you model this?" | Ontology, agent config, lifecycle, curiosity |
| **Phase 2: Operationalize It** | 20 min | "A dealer signs up. Based on your model, how do you get them live?" | State machines, failure modes, ops automation |
| **Phase 3: Own It** | 20 min | Dealer email → systematic optimization | Hypothesis testing, A/B testing, measurement |
| **Bonus** | Time permitting | Equity mining curveball | Model extensibility |
| **Debrief** | 5 min | Write notes immediately | — |

---

## Decision Framework

| Vote | Meaning | When to Give |
|------|---------|--------------|
| **Strong Yes** | Will own this product from Day 1. Hire immediately. | Connects all three phases, thinks like an owner |
| **Yes** | Solid hire, will ramp quickly. | Good fundamentals, some gaps but teachable |
| **No** | Not ready for this role. | Missing key dimensions, implementer mindset |
| **Strong No** | Wrong person entirely. | Red flags, no curiosity, can't think in systems |

**Rule: One No = No Hire.**

- **Let them drive.** Don't lead them through the transitions — see if they connect the dots themselves.
