# Short-Circuit Screen — Senior Sales Outbound (0→1)

**15-20 min | Recruiter-led phone screen | Pass = 2/3 or 3/3**

Use this between the culture fit screen and the full technical interview. If they score 0/3 or 1/3, stop — don't advance to technical.

**For the recruiter:** Read each question exactly as written. You're listening for real stories with real details. No story = stop signal.

---

## Q1: Prompt Engineering in Production

> "Tell me about a time you wrote prompts for an AI system that real users interacted with. What was the hardest part about getting the AI to behave the way you wanted?"

### Pass signals
- Describes a **real product** where they controlled AI behavior through prompts
- Tells a **specific struggle** — edge cases, tone, hallucination, consistency, getting worse at X when improving Y
- Mentions **iterating** — it didn't work the first time, they had to adjust and re-test
- You can tell they've **fought with prompts**, not just written them once

### Stop signals
- Only experience is chatting with ChatGPT or personal side projects
- "It was pretty straightforward, I just told it what to do"
- Can't describe a real system with real users
- Vague — "I've done some prompt engineering" with no specifics

---

## Q2: SQL & Data Analysis

> "Tell me about a time you used SQL to answer a business question. What was the question, what data did you need to pull together, and what did you find?"

### Pass signals
- Describes a **real business question** — not just "I queried a table"
- Mentions **joining data from different sources** or combining multiple tables
- Got to an **actual insight** — something they learned, a decision it informed
- You can tell they're **comfortable** — this is something they do regularly

### Stop signals
- Can't think of a specific example
- Describes a basic lookup ("I got a list of users")
- "I mostly use an ORM, I don't write raw SQL"
- Hesitates, or says "I could learn SQL"

---

## Q3: Building from Zero

> "Tell me about a time you built something from scratch — no existing codebase, no clear spec. How did you decide what to build first?"

### Pass signals
- Has a **real 0→1 story** — built something new, not just added features to an existing product
- Talks about **figuring out what to build** — talked to users, shipped something small, iterated
- Describes **making decisions under uncertainty** — didn't wait for perfect information
- Mentions **shipping fast** and learning from what happened

### Stop signals
- Has never built anything from scratch — always worked on existing codebases
- "I waited for the PM to give me requirements"
- Describes a personal side project with no users or stakes
- No sense of urgency or shipping quickly

---

## How to Score

| Result | Decision |
|--------|----------|
| **3/3** | Strong advance to technical. Tell Haroun: "nailed all three." |
| **2/3** | Advance to technical. Note which question they were weak on. |
| **1/3** | Don't advance. Share notes with Haroun for a final call. |
| **0/3** | Hard no. Don't advance. |

**The most important question is Q1.** This person will own all the Sales AI prompts from day one. If they've never wrestled with getting an LLM to behave in a real product, they're not ready.

---

## Tips for the Recruiter

- **Read the question exactly as written.** Don't paraphrase.
- **Let them talk.** Silence is okay — give them time to think of their story.
- **You're listening for real stories.** Specifics = pass. Generalities = stop.
- **Take notes on the details they give** — project names, what the system did, what went wrong. Haroun will read your notes.
- If they ask a clarifying question, say: "Whatever comes to mind — I'm interested in a specific example from your experience."
